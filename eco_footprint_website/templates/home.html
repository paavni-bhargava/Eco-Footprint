What is Carbon Footprint?

Carbon footprint refers to the total amount of greenhouse gases released into the atmosphere as a result of everyday human activities. These emissions come from the use of electricity, transportation, food consumption, and various lifestyle choices that rely on fossil fuels and energy resources.

Main Sources of Carbon Emissions

Electricity: Power generation from fossil fuels
Transportation: Cars, bikes, buses, and flights
Food: Meat-based diets have higher emissions
Fuel: LPG, petrol, and diesel usage

Why It Matters?

Excessive carbon emissions contribute directly to climate change, rising global temperatures, and increasing environmental instability. They affect air quality, water resources, agriculture, and natural ecosystems, impacting both present and future generations.
